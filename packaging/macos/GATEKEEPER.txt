Carbonyl for macOS is currently UNSIGNED (no Apple Developer ID).

macOS Gatekeeper will block it on first launch ("unidentified developer" or
"Apple could not verify this app is free of malware"). To allow it:

  Option A — System Settings:
    1. Double-click the .pkg. When macOS blocks it, open
       System Settings > Privacy & Security.
    2. Scroll to the message about "carbonyl" and click "Open Anyway",
       then re-open the .pkg.

  Option B — Terminal (clears the download quarantine flag):
       xattr -d com.apple.quarantine ~/Downloads/carbonyl-*.pkg
    then double-click the .pkg again.

After installation, carbonyl is at /usr/local/bin/carbonyl. Run e.g.:

       carbonyl https://example.com

(Ensure /usr/local/bin is on your PATH.)

A signed + notarized installer will replace this once an Apple Developer ID is
available (see ADR-003 and issue #129).
